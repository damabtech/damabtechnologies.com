Why Us?

Our team of experienced technicians is equipped with state-of-the-art air monitoring equipment to measure and analyze the air quality in your space. We test for a wide range of pollutants, including allergens, dust, mold, volatile organic compounds (VOCs), and other harmful particles. Our air monitoring services include:

  • Indoor air quality testing: We test the air inside your home or workplace to identify any potential sources of indoor air pollution and recommend solutions to improve the air quality.
  • Mold testing: We use advanced techniques to detect the presence of mold and identify the type of mold present.
  • Allergen testing: We test for common allergens such as dust mites, pet dander, and pollen to help identify potential triggers for allergies and asthma.
  • Air Monitoring: We use state-of-the-art equipment to monitor the air during and after the asbestos abatement process to ensure that your property is safe for occupancy.
  • VOC Testing: We test for volatile organic compounds, which are emitted as gases from certain solids or liquids, such as paints, cleaning products, and building materials.

Techniques and Methods Used:

The specific methods used can vary depending on the company and the type of pollutants being tested for, but some common air monitoring techniques include:

  • Particle Counting: This involves using a particle counter to measure the number and size of particles in the air. The device can detect particles as small as 0.3 microns and can be used to measure the concentration of pollutants such as dust, pollen, and pet dander.
  • Gas Detection: This involves using a gas analyzer or sensor to detect and measure the concentration of gases such as carbon monoxide, carbon dioxide, and volatile organic compounds (VOCs) in the air.
  • Air Samples: This involves collecting air samples using pumps or other devices, and then analyzing the samples in a laboratory to identify the presence and concentration of pollutants.
  • Microbial Sampling: This technique is used to detect and identify mold and bacteria. Surface samples and air samples are taken and analyzed to identify the type of microorganism present and the concentration of it.

We will take the data collected from the above methods and use it to generate a report that includes detailed information on the air quality, including any potential sources of pollution and recommendations for improving the air quality.
