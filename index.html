Asbestos, Lead And Mold Assessment

  • Asbestos

    Asbestos is a naturally occurring mineral that was widely used in building materials and other products before the dangers of its fibers were fully understood. When asbestos-containing materials are disturbed, tiny fibers can be released into the air, which can be inhaled and can cause serious health problems, including lung cancer, mesothelioma, and asbestosis.

  • Lead

    Lead is a toxic heavy metal that was widely used in building materials, paint, and other products before the dangers of lead exposure were fully understood. When lead-containing materials are disturbed or begin to deteriorate, lead dust and chips can be released into the air, which can be inhaled or ingested, and can cause serious health problems, especially in children and pregnant women, such as developmental delays, learning difficulties and behavioral problems.

  • Mold

    Mold is a type of fungus that can grow on various surfaces in buildings and homes, including walls, ceilings, floors, and furniture. Mold can grow in damp or humid environments, and can cause a variety of health problems, such as allergies, asthma, and respiratory infections. In addition, mold can damage the structure of the building and its contents.
